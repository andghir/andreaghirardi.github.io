Processo

Archivio began as a study of renaissance typefaces, specifically the proportions of Garamond and the ink traps of early metal type. The goal was not to revive a historical model but to distil the principles that made those typefaces timeless: optical balance, rhythmic spacing, and weight contrast that survives reduction.

The typeface ships in four weights, Light, Regular, Medium, and Bold, each with a companion italic. The italic is a true cursive design, not a mechanical slant: the letterforms shift and soften, shifting from authority to warmth without losing coherence.
