Overview

Exhibition identity and wayfinding system for a major contemporary art museum, a graphic language designed to recede in deference to the art, while remaining authoritative enough to orient visitors across six floors.

Approccio

The challenge of museum wayfinding is one of hierarchy: the system must be clear enough to navigate, but quiet enough not to compete with the works on display. The solution was a purely typographic system, no icons, no colour coding, using scale alone to convey hierarchy.

All signage uses a single typeface at two weights. Location information is set large and light; interpretive text is set smaller and regular. The consistency across all touchpoints, from the monumental entrance banners to the small room-number plaques, creates a calm, unified experience.
